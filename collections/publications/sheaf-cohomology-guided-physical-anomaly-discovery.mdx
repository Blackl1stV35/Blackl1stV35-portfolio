abstract: "We present ForgeWM, a framework for identifying missing physical laws from simulation data using sheaf cohomology and persistent homology over hypothesis spaces. The core contribution is threefold. First, we prove that physical regime transitions correspond to non-trivial generators of the first cohomology group $H^0(X,\mathcal{F})$ of a physical observation sheaf, providing a formal algebraic characterisation of where existing axiom systems fail. Second, we prove that persistent cohomology on finite simulation samples converges to the true sheaf cohomology, with an explicit sample complexity bound. Third, we demonstrate that residual-guided anomaly detection identifies the samples most informative for symbolic recovery of unknown constitutive laws.

Experiments on synthetic structural mechanics data validate the cohomological transition recovery with 0.07% error against a known ground truth. An ablation study shows that topology-guided sampling requires 1.84× fewer FEM evaluations to converge than random sampling ($p = 0.00036$). We further show that when the feature space cannot encode the governing parameters (feature ceiling $R^2 = 0.70$), the framework correctly diagnoses the information-limited regime rather than producing spurious results. All proofs are complete; all experiments are reproducible on a single GPU."
