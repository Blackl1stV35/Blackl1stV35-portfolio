abstract: "Loyalty programs represent a substantial corporate investment in Southeast Asia, yet a structurally distinct customer segment—loyalty-agnostic customers (LACs)—transacts regularly while exhibiting near-zero engagement with loyalty and customer relationship management (CRM) mechanics. This study introduces and operationalizes the LAC construct, defined by a behavioral signature of high purchase frequency, low CRM responsiveness, high promotional purchase ratio, and near-zero loyalty redemption, irrespective of satisfaction. Drawing on a calibrated synthetic behavioral dataset of 50,000 customer records across six digital service industries in the Southeast Asian market, the study tests three hypotheses: (H1) LAC density varies significantly across industries; (H2) behavioral transactional signals classify LAC customers with high accuracy; and (H3) LAC customers exhibit significantly stronger response to contextual marketing triggers than to relational triggers. All three hypotheses are supported. LAC density ranges from 32.7% (banking/digital wallet) to 58.2% (e-commerce/marketplace), with 15 of 15 pairwise industry comparisons significant after Bonferroni correction (χ² = 1,597.81, p < .001, Cramér’s V = .179). A logistic regression classifier achieves area under the curve (AUC) = .9997 (SE = .0001) using five transactional features. Contextual trigger uplift for LAC customers (+30.8%) exceeds relational uplift (+2.2%) by a ratio of 13.7× (Mann-Whitney U, p < .001). Implications for marketing resource allocation and CRM strategy in high-platform-penetration emerging markets are discussed."
