abstract: "Poor psychological well-being among the urban workforce imposes measurable costs on labour productivity, absenteeism, and occupational performance, yet the dominant mechanisms — whether financial cognitive load, perceived social standing, or digital media behaviour — remain underspecified in the Thai urban context. 
This study examines the behavioural-economic determinants of psychological well-being among 411 working-age adults in Bangkok (aged 18–44, spanning four industry sectors) using the WHO-5 Well-Being Index (0–100) as the dependent variable. Three OLS regression models are estimated with heteroscedasticity-robust standard errors. Model M1 (R² = 0.369) establishes that passive social media exposure (PasExp, unconditional β = −9.62, p < 0.001), psychological scarcity (PsySca, β = −3.68, p < 0.001), and subjective social status (SubStat, β = 3.27, p < 0.001) are the dominant predictors of well-being, while objective household income is not statistically significant. Model M2 confirms that occupation sector adds no independent explanatory power (ΔAIC = +5.0). 
Model M3 introduces a PasExp × SubStat interaction term (β = −2.678, p < 0.001), improving model fit (R² = 0.405, AIC = 3,568) and revealing a social comparison indifference point at SubStat ≈ 3.22: above this threshold — covering the majority of the sample — higher passive exposure increasingly harms well-being (average marginal effect at mean SubStat = −8.08 WHO-5 points); below it, exposure may carry modest aspirational benefits. All three pre-specified hypotheses are supported. Back-of-envelope estimates suggest that status-calibrated programme targeting yields approximately 5.6 additional WHO-5 points per contact over uniform delivery — equivalent to approximately 56,000 aggregate well-being points across a programme reaching 10,000 contacts at no additional cost.
The findings support a dual-pathway framework in which financial cognitive load and upward digital comparison independently reduce well-being, moderated by perceived social rank. Policy implications are operationalised through three Bangkok welfare programmes mapped to the three interaction-surface zones: the State Welfare Card (low-status, high-PsySca stratum), the BMA Tor Tuem Jai digital well-being platform (mid-to-high status, above the indifference point), and the Khon La Khrueng Plus scheme (transitional middle-income range).
