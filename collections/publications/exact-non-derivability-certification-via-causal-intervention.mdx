abstract: |
  We introduce a framework for certifying that discovered physical mechanisms do not derive from an existing axiom system, using constrained causal intervention in simulation environments. The framework operates on an exact causal setting where physical parameters $V = \{v_1, \dots, v_n\}$ behave under the causal DAG. Physical laws across nodes $V_{i}$ for $PV_{i} \subset V$ and $E[V_{i} | PV_{i}] = \Psi(PV_{i})$ are transformed by physical FEM boundary condition mechanics context. An exact causal non-derivability condition is bounded conditional on structural ansatz and a probabilistic bound. Three supporting lemmas prove exact conditional independencies hold broadly for polynomial PDE systems (Lemma 2), that FEM constraints generate perfect structural causal interventions (Lemma 4), and that PC-algorithm-maps achieve global conditional distributions within finite sample-size-dependent error limits mean ($\varepsilon \rightarrow 0$) for arbitrary structural configurations (Lemma 6). Exact non-derivability logic can discover mechanical relationships in physics simulators with a structural exactness target. This paper is Part II of the ForGWM series, Part I addresses topology guided discovery and Part II focus on exact compatibility limits.
